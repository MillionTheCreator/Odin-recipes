    • Fufu With Grilled Chicken

    Fufu-traditional West/Central-African food

    Fufu:

    Ingredients:

    • 2 cups cassava flour or yam flour
    • Water

      • Instructions:

      Prepare the Fufu
    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
    • Gradually add the cassava flour or yam flour while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
    • Keep stirring until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth, similar to mashed potatoes. This may take about 10-15 minutes.
    • Reduce the heat to low and continue stirring for a few more minutes until the fufu is well-cooked and holds its shape.
      • 2. Serve the Fufu
    • Fufu is typically served as a side dish with various soups or stews. It's often enjoyed by scooping a portion with your fingers and using it to scoop up the soup or stew.

    Saka Saka (Spinach Stew):

    Ingredients:

    • 2 bunches of fresh spinach, washed and chopped
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2-3 tablespoons palm oil
    • 1 cup water or broth
    • Salt and pepper to taste

      • Instructions:

      Prepare the Saka Saka
    • In a pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat.
    • Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
    • Add the chopped spinach and stir well.
    • Pour in the water or broth and let the spinach cook down until tender.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.

    Grilled Chicken

    Ingredients:

    • 4 chicken leg quarters
    • Marinade:
      • 4 cup olive oil
      • 2 cloves garlic, minced
      • 1 teaspoon paprika
      • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
      • Salt and pepper to taste

      Instructions:

      Marinate the Chicken:
      • In a bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, paprika, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
      • Coat the chicken leg quarters with the marinade and let them marinate for at least 1 hour or preferably overnight.
      Grill the Chicken:
      • Preheat a grill to medium-high heat.
      • Grill the marinated chicken leg quarters for about 6-8 minutes on each side or until fully cooked and the internal temperature reaches 74°C (165°F).
      • Remove from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

    Serving Suggestions

    Serve a portion of fufu alongside a bowl of Saka Saka (Spinach Stew) and a piece of grilled chicken for a delicious and balanced West African meal. You can also add other traditional West African side dishes like fried plantains, jollof rice, or groundnut soup to complete the meal. Enjoy your flavorful West African feast! Feel free to customize the recipes and sides to suit your taste preferences.
